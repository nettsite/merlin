Quotes & Credit Notes

Quotes and credit notes are the two remaining billing document types. Both reuse the shared Document / DocumentLine models — there are no separate tables — differing only in document_type, number prefix, and status machine. Neither type ever posts to the ledger directly.

Numbers are generated by the HasDocumentNumber trait (PREFIX-YEAR-NNNNN); prefixes and status lists live in config/documents.php. Both pages are Livewire single-file components with a list + detail flyout, inline line editing, and status-driven action buttons.

Quote lifecycle

Statuses: draft → sent → accepted → converted, with declined and expired as alternative terminals. All transitions go through DocumentService:

  • sendQuote() — emails the quote and moves it to sent.
  • acceptQuote() / declineQuote() / expireQuote() — record the client's response.
  • convertQuoteToInvoice() — creates a new sales_invoice document copying the party and lines, gives it its own SINV number, links the two documents, and marks the quote converted. Returns the new invoice.

Credit note lifecycle

Statuses: draft → issued → applied, with voided as the cancellation terminal:

  • issueCreditNote() — finalises the draft; amounts are fixed from this point.
  • applyCreditNote(Document $creditNote, Document $invoice, ...) — applies the credit against a sales invoice, reducing its outstanding balance, and links the documents.
  • voidDocument() — cancels; the record remains for audit.
Header edits only in draft Both page components allow header and line editing only while the document is in draft. Every mutating Livewire action re-checks authorization independently, per the project's Livewire security rules (#[Locked] IDs, authorize() in each action).

Relationship to accounts and reports

Quotes never appear in financial reports. Credit notes affect reported income once applied, through the invoice balance they reduce. The GL entry story remains owned by the sales-invoice posting flow — see Sales Invoices.
