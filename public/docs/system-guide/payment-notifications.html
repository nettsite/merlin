Payment Notifications

Payment notifications are receipts and payment confirmations — PayPal receipts, FNB Connect confirmations, Payfast notifications — that arrive through the same ingestion path as purchase invoices. Merlin classifies them as a distinct document type, matches them to the purchase invoice they settle, and consolidates the pair into a single document carrying both source files.

Document type

Payment notifications use document_type = payment_notification with number prefix PMTN (see config/documents.php). Classification happens during LLM extraction: the prompt distinguishes a payment receipt from an invoice, and the pipeline routes the document to PaymentNotificationProcessingService instead of the invoice pipeline.

Matching

PaymentNotificationMatcher (app/Modules/Purchasing/Services/) does the pairing. Matching runs in both directions:

  • findInvoiceMatch() — a freshly processed payment notification searches existing purchase invoices. Only invoices issued on or before the payment date, within a 30-day window (MAX_DATE_WINDOW_DAYS), are candidates.
  • findPaymentMatch() — the reciprocal check. A freshly processed purchase invoice searches pending payment notifications (status received) dated on or after its issue date, within the same 30-day window. This handles the receipt-arrives-first ordering.

Each candidate is scored on amount and supplier/name similarity; the best match above threshold is returned as {document, confidence, reason}. The reason string is recorded for auditability.

Consolidation

On a successful match the notification is merged into the invoice document rather than kept as a sibling: the invoice ends up carrying both PDFs on its source_document media collection (the bill and the proof of payment), and the standalone notification document is absorbed. The invoice detail view lists both files.

Foreign-currency correction

For foreign-currency invoices, the payment notification shows the amount actually charged in base currency — better data than the daily rate from ExchangeRateService. When the matched notification is confirmed, Merlin corrects the invoice's base-currency figures to the settled amount. The correction is deliberately gated on confirmation status: an unconfirmed notification never rewrites invoice amounts.

No GL payment entry — ever A matched payment notification documents that payment occurred; it never records a payment transaction in the ledger. Recording the actual cash movement is the bank-statement pipeline's job — the bank statement remains the single source of truth for money leaving the account. This avoids double-counting when the same payment later appears on a statement.

Duplicate invoice merging

DuplicateInvoiceMerger handles the adjacent problem of the same invoice arriving twice (supplier resends, reminder copies). When a newly processed invoice matches an existing one on supplier, number, and amounts, the new file is merged into the existing document's media instead of creating a second Document row. InvoiceProcessingService invokes the merger as part of processing.

Testing

Feature coverage lives in the purchase-invoice test suites: matcher scoring and window bounds, receipt-first and invoice-first orderings, the confirmed-only forex gate, and duplicate-resend merging. When changing the matcher, keep the date-window queries on whereDate() — raw string comparison misses same-day rows on SQLite.
