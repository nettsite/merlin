Client Portal

The client portal gives a client's contact person their own login — separate from staff accounts — to view and eventually pay their invoices online. The authentication layer is live; the invoice-viewing pages are the next build phase.

Authentication

Portal users authenticate against a dedicated portal guard, entirely separate from the staff web guard. Routes live in routes/portal.php under the /portal prefix:

RouteMiddlewarePurpose
/portal/loginguest:portalPortal login page.
/portal/set-password/{token}guest:portalInvite acceptance — the contact sets their password via a tokened link.
POST /portal/logoutauth:portalSession logout.

Invitations

Access is invitation-only: PortalInviteService (app/Modules/Billing/Services/) issues a set-password link to an existing contact person. There is no open self-registration — that's a deliberately parked decision (see ROADMAP.md) to keep the attack surface small.

Rate limiting is the portal's own job The portal guard inherits none of Breeze's login throttling. Any portal auth action (login, set-password) must include its own RateLimiter block — this is an established project rule, not an optimisation.

What's next

Phase C3 adds /portal/invoices/{invoice} — authenticated invoice viewing, followed by online payment. Related parked items (multi-party client switcher for contacts who represent several parties, supplier self-service) are documented in ROADMAP.md.
