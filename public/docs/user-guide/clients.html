Clients

Clients are the businesses and individuals you sell to and invoice. You need to have a client set up before you can create a sales invoice for them.

Adding a client

  1. Go to Clients in the navigation.
  2. Click Add Client.
  3. Fill in their details and save.

Client details

FieldNotes
Legal nameRequired. The registered or full name of the client.
Trading nameOptional. The name they use day-to-day if different.
EmailOptional. Used when sending invoices by email.
PhoneOptional.
NotesAny notes you want to keep about this client.
StatusActive, Pending, or Inactive.
Default payment termThe payment terms that apply to this client's invoices. When you create a sales invoice for them, this is pre-filled automatically.
Default receivable accountThe account used to track what this client owes you. Pre-filled on their invoices.
Billing addressAppears on PDF invoices you send them.

Contacts

You can add contact people for a client — useful when the client is a company and you need to record the name and email of the person who handles invoices. When sending an invoice, you can address it to a specific contact.

To add a contact, open the client and use the Add Contact form. Each contact has a first name, last name, email, and phone.

Client summary columns

The client list shows Total invoiced and Total owing columns. Use the period filter at the top of the page to change the date range — for example, to see how much a client was invoiced this month versus the full financial year. "Total owing" is the amount on invoices that haven't been fully paid.

Viewing a client

Click View on any client to open their detail page. This shows the full invoice history for that client — every sales invoice, its amount, status, due date, and whether it has been paid. The running balance at the top shows the total outstanding across all unpaid invoices.

You can also edit the client's details and billing defaults from this page.

Editing a client

Click Edit from the client list or from the client's detail page. Update their details and save. If a client is no longer active, set their status to Inactive — their past invoices remain on record.
