Users & Access

Merlin uses a role-based access system. Each user is assigned a role, and roles determine what that person can see and do in the application. This lets you give staff only the access they need.

Adding a user

  1. Go to Settings › Users.
  2. Click Add User.
  3. Enter their full name, email address, and a temporary password. Ask them to change their password after their first login.
  4. Assign them a role.
  5. Save.

A user without a role cannot log in to Merlin.

Roles

Roles are named sets of permissions. You manage roles at Settings › Roles. Merlin comes with a built-in Administrator role that has full access to everything.

You can create additional roles for your team. For example:

  • A Bookkeeper role that can upload and review invoices but cannot post them
  • A Manager role that can approve and post invoices
  • An Accounts admin role with full access to billing and accounts

To create a role, go to Settings › Roles, click Add Role, give it a name, and tick the permissions you want it to include.

Key permissions

The most commonly configured permissions are:

PermissionWhat it allows
View purchase invoicesSee the purchase invoice list and open individual invoices
Review invoicesMark an invoice as reviewed
Authorise invoicesApprove an invoice for posting
Post invoicesPost an invoice to your accounts (the final step)
Reprocess invoicesDelete and redo the AI extraction on an invoice
Send sales invoicesEmail a sales invoice to a client
Record paymentsRecord a client payment against a sales invoice
Void sales invoicesCancel a sales invoice permanently

There are also permissions for viewing, creating, editing, and deleting records in each area of the application (suppliers, clients, accounts, and so on). These are all set per role.

Administrator access

The Administrator role has full access to everything, including settings and user management. Only give this role to people who need to manage the application itself.

Changing a user's role

Open the user record in Settings › Users and change their role. The change takes effect immediately — the user's access changes on their next page load.

Deactivating a user

If someone leaves, you can remove their role to prevent them from logging in, or delete their account entirely. Their past activity (invoices they reviewed, approved, etc.) remains on record.
