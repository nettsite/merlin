Quotes

Quotes are price proposals you send to clients before any work is done or goods are supplied. A quote looks like an invoice — client, line items, amounts — but it doesn't affect your accounts. If the client accepts, you convert it to a sales invoice with one click.

Creating a quote

  1. Go to Quotes and click New Quote.
  2. Choose the client and set the quote date.
  3. Add line items — description, quantity, unit price, and VAT.

The quote is created in Draft status and gets a quote number automatically (e.g. QUO-2026-00012). You can edit the header and lines freely while it's a draft.

Quote statuses

StatusWhat it means
DraftBeing prepared. Not yet sent to the client.
SentEmailed to the client. Waiting for a response.
AcceptedThe client has accepted. Ready to convert to an invoice.
ConvertedAn invoice has been created from this quote. Final state.
DeclinedThe client turned it down. Final state.
ExpiredThe quote lapsed without a response. Final state.

Sending a quote

Open the quote and click Send. Merlin emails the quote to the client's contact and moves it to Sent.

Recording the outcome

  • Mark Accepted — the client said yes. The quote is now ready to convert.
  • Decline — the client said no. You'll be asked to confirm; declined quotes can't be reopened.
  • Expire — the quote lapsed without an answer.

Converting to an invoice

Once a quote is accepted, click Convert to Invoice. Merlin creates a new sales invoice with the same client and line items, gives it its own invoice number, and marks the quote as Converted. From there the invoice behaves like any other sales invoice — you send it, track payment, and post it.

Quotes never touch your accounts Nothing about a quote is posted to your ledger — not when it's created, sent, or accepted. Only the sales invoice created by conversion affects your books.

Filtering and searching

Use the status tabs at the top of the list (All, Draft, Sent, Accepted, Converted, Declined, Expired) to filter, and the search box to find quotes by number or client.
