Accounts

The chart of accounts is where all your financial transactions are recorded. Every line on every invoice — whether you're posting a purchase or recording a sale — ends up in an account. The accounts then feed into your reports.

How accounts are organised

Accounts sit in a three-level structure:

  • Account type — the broadest category: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Income, or Expenses.
  • Account group — a subcategory within a type. For example, "Current Assets" under Assets, or "Operating Expenses" under Expenses.
  • Account — the individual account, such as "Telephone & Internet" under Operating Expenses.

When you allocate a line on an invoice, you're picking an individual account.

The default chart of accounts

Merlin comes with a default chart of accounts as a starting point. It covers the most common account types for a small business:

Code rangeWhat it covers
1000sAssets — bank accounts, accounts receivable, prepayments
2000sLiabilities — accounts payable, VAT/GST owed, loans
3000sEquity — owner's capital and retained earnings
4000sIncome — revenue from sales and services
5000sExpenses — operating costs such as telephone, professional services, cloud hosting

You can rename accounts, add new ones, or deactivate ones you don't use.

Adding an account

  1. Go to Accounts.
  2. Click Add Account.
  3. Choose the account group it belongs to.
  4. Give it a code (e.g. 5150) and a name (e.g. "Software Subscriptions").
  5. Leave Allow direct posting ticked — this lets you post invoice lines to it. Turn it off only for summary or parent accounts that you don't post to directly.
  6. Save.

Account groups

Account groups are managed under Accounting › Account Groups. A group belongs to one of the five account types (Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Income, Expenses). You can add new groups if your chart of accounts needs more categories than the defaults provide.

Viewing an account's transactions

Click an account in the list (or its view icon) to open the account's transaction history — every posted line that hit the account, with dates, descriptions, and amounts. Use this to answer "what exactly is in this account?" when a report figure looks off.

Deactivating an account

If you no longer use an account, set it to inactive rather than deleting it. Inactive accounts won't appear in the account picker when reviewing invoices, but all past transactions posted to them remain on record.
