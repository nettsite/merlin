Credit Notes

A credit note reduces what a client owes you — because you overcharged, goods were returned, or you're settling a dispute. It's the opposite of a sales invoice: instead of adding to the client's balance, it subtracts from it.

Creating a credit note

  1. Go to Credit Notes and click New Credit Note.
  2. Choose the client and set the date.
  3. Add line items describing what is being credited, with amounts and VAT.

The credit note starts in Draft status with its own number (e.g. CRN-2026-00005). Edit the header and lines freely while it's a draft.

Credit note statuses

StatusWhat it means
DraftBeing prepared. Amounts can still be changed.
IssuedFinalised and issued to the client. Ready to apply against an invoice.
AppliedApplied against a sales invoice, reducing what's owed. Final state.
VoidedCancelled. Final state.

Issuing

When the details are right, click Issue. This finalises the credit note — the amounts are now fixed.

Applying to an invoice

Click Apply on an issued credit note and choose the sales invoice it offsets. The credit reduces the outstanding balance of that invoice and the credit note moves to Applied.

Voiding

If a credit note was created in error, click Void. Voided credit notes stay in the list for your records but have no effect on any balance.

Don't edit — credit Once a sales invoice has been sent or posted, don't try to change it. Issue a credit note instead. That keeps a clean, auditable trail of what was charged and what was corrected.

Filtering and searching

Use the status tabs (All, Draft, Issued, Applied, Voided) to filter the list, and the search box to find credit notes by number or client.
