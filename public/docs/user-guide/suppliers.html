Suppliers

Suppliers are the businesses and people you buy from. Every purchase invoice you upload needs to be linked to a supplier. You can add suppliers manually, or Merlin can create them automatically when it spots a name on an uploaded invoice that it doesn't recognise.

Adding a supplier

  1. Go to Suppliers in the navigation.
  2. Click Add Supplier.
  3. Fill in the supplier's details and click Save.

Supplier details

FieldNotes
Legal nameRequired. The registered name of the business.
Trading nameOptional. The name they trade under if different from their legal name.
EmailOptional. Mainly for reference.
PhoneOptional.
NotesOptional. Any notes you want to keep about this supplier.
StatusActive, Pending, or Inactive. Only Active suppliers are matched when processing invoices.
Default payable accountOptional. The account invoices from this supplier are usually posted to. Merlin will suggest this account when reviewing their invoices.
Default payment termOptional. Used to calculate due dates on their invoices.

New suppliers found on invoices

When you upload an invoice and Merlin can't match the supplier name to anyone in your list, it creates a new supplier automatically with a Pending status. You'll see pending suppliers in the Suppliers list.

To confirm a pending supplier:

  1. Open the supplier from the list.
  2. Check that the name, tax number, and other details are correct — Merlin fills in what it found on the invoice.
  3. Change the status to Active.
  4. Set a default payable account and payment term if you have standard arrangements with them.
  5. Save.

Future invoices from this supplier will be matched automatically.

Editing a supplier

Click any supplier in the list to open their details. Make your changes and save. You can also set a supplier to Inactive if you no longer use them — their past invoices stay on record.

The supplier page

Clicking a supplier's name opens their full profile, which shows their contact details and a list of recent invoices from them. Use this to check their history or look up a specific past invoice.
