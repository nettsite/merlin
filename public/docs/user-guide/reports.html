Reports

Merlin's reports are based on transactions that have been posted to your accounts. They give you a picture of your income, expenses, and financial position, as well as how well the invoice processing is performing.

All reports are under Reports in the navigation. Opening Reports takes you to the Income Statement by default.

Income Statement

Shows your income and expenses side by side for the period, with a net profit/loss figure at the bottom. Two columns are shown: month to date and financial year to date.

Use this report to see whether the business is profitable and to compare monthly performance against the year as a whole.

Balance Sheet

Shows your assets, liabilities, and equity at a point in time. The report validates that assets equal liabilities plus equity — if it doesn't balance, there is a data entry issue to investigate.

Use this for a snapshot of the financial health of the business.

Trial Balance

Lists every account with its debit and credit totals for the period. The totals must equal each other. Use this report to check the integrity of your books or to prepare for an audit.

Income by Account

Shows total income grouped by GL account over the period. Use this to see which income categories are driving revenue — for example, how much came from consulting versus product sales.

Income by Client

Shows total income grouped by client over the period. Use this to see which clients are generating the most revenue and to spot changes in client activity.

Expenses by Account

Shows your total spend in each expense account over the period. Use this to see where your money goes — how much on telephone, professional services, cloud hosting, and so on.

The figures come from posted purchase invoices only. Invoices that haven't been posted yet don't appear.

Expenses by Supplier

Shows your total spend per supplier over the period. Use this to review your largest suppliers or prepare for supplier negotiations.

AI Performance

Shows how the invoice reading has been performing. Useful if you want to understand how often invoices are going to manual review, how confident the AI has been on average, or how much it's costing in API usage.

The report shows:

  • How many invoices were processed in the period
  • The average confidence score across all extractions
  • How many needed a second attempt (the AI tried once, wasn't confident enough, and tried again with a more capable model)
  • The total number of tokens used (which relates to API cost)

Date ranges

Reports default to the current financial year. You can change the date range using the filter at the top of each report. The financial year start month is set in Settings under the Accounting tab — if you haven't set it, check with your administrator.
