Recurring Invoices

Recurring invoices are templates that generate a real invoice automatically on a schedule. Use them for monthly retainers, subscription fees, rent, or any invoice that repeats on the same pattern.

How it works

You set up a recurring invoice template with the client, line items, and schedule. Every morning, Merlin checks whether any templates are due. When the next invoice date arrives, Merlin creates a full sales invoice from the template and advances the schedule to the next date. If auto-send is on, it emails the invoice to the client at the same time.

Setting up a recurring invoice

  1. Go to Recurring Invoices.
  2. Click New.
  3. Select the client and fill in the details.
  4. Add the line items that will appear on each generated invoice.
  5. Set the frequency and start date.
  6. Turn on Auto-send if you want the invoice emailed automatically when it's generated.
  7. Save and set the status to Active.

Fields

FieldNotes
ClientRequired. The client this invoice is for.
Payment termUsed to calculate the due date on each generated invoice.
Receivable accountThe account used to track what the client owes.
FrequencyMonthly, quarterly, or annually.
Billing dayThe day of the month the invoice is generated. For example, set to 1 to generate on the 1st of each month.
Start dateThe date of the first invoice.
End dateOptional. If set, no invoices are generated after this date.
CurrencyThe currency for generated invoices.
Auto-sendIf on, the generated invoice is emailed to the client automatically.
NotesInternal notes — not shown on the invoice.
TermsPayment terms text shown on the invoice.
FooterFooter text shown on the invoice.

Statuses

StatusWhat it means
ActiveInvoices will be generated on schedule.
PausedGeneration is temporarily stopped. Change back to Active to resume.
CompletedThe schedule has ended (either the end date has passed, or you set it manually). No more invoices will be generated.

Finding the generated invoices

Each invoice Merlin generates from a recurring template appears in the Sales Invoices list as a normal invoice. You can view, send, and record payment against it just like any other sales invoice.
